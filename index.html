Case Study 01

Proposal V2 — Rebuilding the proposal flow into a structured, multi-signatory workflow

From a linear form with single-client signing into an inline builder with multi-signatory acceptance and signature tracking.

Workflow redesign B2B sales Signature tracking Customization

Problem

The original proposal builder was a linear form. Adding services, packages, add-ons, and tax meant switching across screens, and signing was limited to a single client with no signature audit trail. Clients with multi-stakeholder deals didn't have a clean way to use the flow.

What I did

  • Gathered the recurring problems from clients and support — context-switching pain in the builder, single-signatory limitation, lack of audit info on signatures.
  • Researched competitor proposal tools and standalone e-signature products to see how they handled inline service selection and multi-signatory acceptance.
  • Worked out with engineering what was realistically shippable given the team's capacity, and broke the rebuild into versions instead of one large release.
  • Wrote the PRD, coordinated with design, QA, and engineering through delivery, and ensured each version shipped on time.

What shipped

  • Inline service selection — services, packages, add-ons, and tax configurable directly inside the proposal.
  • Multi-signatory support (up to 5) with primary client logic and clear acceptance rules.
  • Secure public links with IP and timestamp tracking on every signature event.
  • Configurable reminders for stuck proposals.
  • Customization controls so agencies could match their own branding.

Outcome

The new flow removed the context-switching that came up most often in support, and gave clients a way to send proposals to multiple decision-makers with a record of who signed and when — something they previously had to handle outside the platform.

My role: PM on the feature — gathered client problems, did competitor and tool research, wrote the PRD, worked with design and engineering through versions, and coordinated delivery with QA, marketing, and support.
Case Study 02

Company Accounts (B2B) — Letting agencies manage companies, not just contacts

Adding a company-level layer above contacts so agencies could collaborate with multiple people inside a single client business.

B2B accounts Client management Client request Cross-functional delivery

Problem

Agencies on the platform serve businesses, but the system treated every client as a single contact. There was no clean way to keep multiple people from the same client company connected — agencies were creating duplicate contacts for the same company, and sending proposals or invoices at the contact level even when the work was really for the business.

What I did

  • Heard the problem repeatedly from agency clients during meetings and from the support team's tickets — same company, multiple contacts, no clean way to group them.
  • Researched how other CRM and agency tools structure companies vs contacts, and which of those patterns were realistic for our user base.
  • Worked with engineering to figure out what was shippable with the resources we had — kept the model simpler than full org hierarchies and broke the work into versions.
  • Wrote the PRD, coordinated with design and engineering, supported UAT, and worked with the business and support teams on rollout.

What shipped

  • Company as a first-class entity with multiple contacts grouped under it.
  • Ability to send proposals and invoices at the company level.
  • Primary contact logic so it's clear who's responsible for billing and decisions.
  • Backward compatibility so existing contact-only clients kept working.

Outcome

Agencies could now represent their actual client structure in the platform instead of working around it with duplicate contacts. Proposals and invoices started flowing at the company level for clients that needed it.

My role: PM on the feature — gathered client and support input, did competitor research, scoped versions with engineering, wrote the PRD, and worked with the team through delivery and rollout.
Case Study 03

Credit System — Letting clients top up once and use credits across services

A high-request feature solving a recurring purchase friction — clients buying the same low-ticket services again and again.

Billing High client request Feature enhancement Payment integration

Problem

This came up as a high-priority feature request. The pain point was repeat purchasing — clients were going through the full purchase flow again and again for the same kinds of services. A credit-based model was a more viable fit: buy credits once, then use them against services over time.

What I did

  • Took the recurring client request and broke down what a credit-based model would actually need: a way to purchase credits with payment, a place to hold the balance, and a way to redeem them against services.
  • Researched how other platforms handle credit purchasing, balance display, and redemption flows.
  • Worked with engineering on what could ship first vs in later versions, given resource constraints.
  • Wrote the PRD, coordinated with design and engineering through delivery, and worked with support and the business team on launch.

What shipped

  • A credit purchase flow with payment integration.
  • A credit balance per client account, visible inside the platform.
  • Redemption at checkout — credits applied against eligible services with a clear remaining-balance view.
  • Admin controls for managing balances and adjustments.

Outcome

Clients could top up once and use credits across services instead of repeating the full purchase flow for every low-ticket transaction. The repeat-purchase friction that drove the request in the first place was no longer the blocker.

My role: PM on the feature — translated the high-priority client request into a workable scope, did tool research, wrote the PRD, broke the build into versions with engineering, and coordinated with design, QA, and the business team through launch.
